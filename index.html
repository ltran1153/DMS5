Project Speculation

Purpose:

My initial ideation for this project’s creative tool is a text generator that allows users to quickly and easily create pieces of literature based on a couple of chosen words. This function allows users to have an input in the literature they read without the need to think and write from scratch. An example of how this tool could work is the Mad Libs-style stories from thewordfinder.com, which allows users to blindly fill in words into blank spaces of a story, often resulting in nonsensical scenarios. The poem generator from poem-generator.org.uk is an example of a generative tool capable of creating poems from scratch with just a few inputs from the user. This allows users to see their words put into an artistic and evocative piece of literature. Both of these examples show my creative tool’s potential for assisting users with generating a piece of literature without the need for much experience or effort.

Values:

The value of my creative tool is not in the ability to generate literature itself, but rather, as a great source of entertainment, both individually and in a group setting. User input pieces of literature often provide a great source of humor, and the thrill of seeing how one’s input impacted the writing in a social setting is often very enjoyable. The tile flipping toy in ash-k.itch.io/tile-toy shows the value of how one’s small input can turn into a very cool piece of art, and be able to share that cool creation for others to see. My tool aims to mirror that kind of value, but in the context of humor. My type of creative tool is proven to have value by the existence of Mad Libs being widely popular; however, that also means my idea is currently unoriginal and will not provide value over other alternatives. My solution to this problem is to make the tool stand out through its presentation; by gamifying my tool, I will make the audience feel like they are playing with it rather than generating. The inspiration behind this is the Wigglypaint from internet-janitor.itch.io/wigglypaint, a painting software that stands out for its video game-like design and interactions. Very basic digital painting options in this software provide users with playful and stimulating feedback, making drawing here feel fun, whereas it is normally a very meticulous and serious hobby. My experimentation with feedback design also proves this idea; through the concept of gemefying, I was able to make pressing a button give the users a sense of progression and make the action feel more interactive than it should. The value of having a fun and playful tool packaged with a fun and playful presentation is making the experience a video game, not just a software to do a job.

Framing:

For my creative tool’s framing, I am considering the scope and how to use the tool’s constraints to its advantage. This tool is unable to create entire pieces of literature from scratch, therefore I will need to preset several writings that are chosen randomly so that users will get something different for the duration of their use. Additionally, I aim to add a restriction to words by only allowing users to select words from a small list. This restriction can both bolster creativity, providing words that the user may never think of, and add personality that fits the tool’s presentation and aesthetic. Concrete Nest from https://gamesforcrows.itch.io/concrete-nest is my inspiration for how I could make restrictions feel not like a limitation of the tool, but a feature that adds personality to the project. The words in Concrete Nest are varied enough that, for the duration of my visit, I did not see words overlap. Some of the words were long, complex, and flowery, which fits the serene and contemplative atmosphere to make using this tool feel like creating a delicate piece of art. The information design experiment I did is personal experience that restrictions combined with presentation makes the design feel deliberate and creative. The restriction with this project was the alien-sounding keyboard; by creating a space-themed design around that concept, the restriction now becomes more purposeful and enhances the overall experiment. I believe this concept will translate well through my gamefied tool, making users feel like they are a character inside the video game.
